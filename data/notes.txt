Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a subfield of Artificial Intelligence (AI) focused on understanding, interpreting, and generating human language.
The goals of NLP include understanding language, generating language, and bridging human communication with machine understanding.

The morphological level of NLP deals with the structure of words, including prefixes, roots, and suffixes.
The lexical level of NLP focuses on word meaning and vocabulary.
The syntactic level analyzes sentence structure using grammar rules.
Semantic processing determines the meaning of a sentence.
The pragmatic level understands context and real-world meaning.

A typical NLP pipeline includes text collection, cleaning, tokenization, stemming, lemmatization, part-of-speech tagging, and named entity recognition.
Tokenization splits text into smaller units such as words or sentences.
Stemming reduces words to their root forms, such as playing to play and running to run.
Lemmatization uses dictionary knowledge and returns linguistically valid base forms.
Part-of-speech tagging assigns grammatical tags such as noun, verb, and adjective.
Named Entity Recognition identifies entities such as person, location, and organization names.

Bag of Words represents text as word frequency and ignores word order.
TF-IDF measures word importance by balancing term frequency against document frequency.
Word embeddings are dense vector representations that capture semantic meaning.
In Word2Vec, CBOW predicts a target word from context, while Skip-gram predicts context from a target word.
A famous word vector analogy is king minus man plus woman approximately equals queen.
Contextual embeddings from models like BERT, GPT, and RoBERTa change word representation based on context.

A language model predicts the probability of word sequences.
Major types of language models include statistical n-gram models, neural models such as RNN and LSTM, and transformer models.
A unigram contains one word, a bigram contains two words, and a trigram contains three words.
A bigram is a sequence of two words.

Common machine learning algorithms in NLP include Naive Bayes, Logistic Regression, SVM, and Decision Trees.
Common NLP tasks include sentiment analysis, spam detection, and topic classification.
RNNs are useful for sequential data but can suffer from the vanishing gradient problem.
LSTM improves RNN behavior by preserving longer-term dependencies.
GRU is a simplified gated recurrent architecture related to LSTM.

Transformers revolutionized NLP by modeling long-range dependencies efficiently with attention.
The key concept in transformers is the attention mechanism.
BERT is commonly used for language understanding tasks.
GPT is commonly used for text generation tasks.
T5 uses a text-to-text framework for multiple NLP tasks.

Text classification assigns labels to text, such as spam or not spam.
Sentiment analysis detects positive, negative, or neutral emotion in text.
Machine translation converts text from one language to another, such as English to Hindi.
Question answering systems return direct answers for natural-language questions.
Text summarization produces a shorter version of long text while preserving key meaning.
Chatbots simulate human conversation for support and assistance.

Classification evaluation commonly uses accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score.
NLP evaluation often also uses BLEU for translation quality and ROUGE for summarization quality.

Common NLP challenges include ambiguity, sarcasm, context understanding, and multilingual complexity.
Ambiguity means one word can have multiple meanings, such as bat as an animal or sports equipment.
Sarcasm is difficult because literal wording may differ from intended meaning.
Context understanding remains difficult when background knowledge is required.
Multilingual processing is hard because languages differ in grammar, script, and usage patterns.

Applications of NLP include chatbots, search engines, voice assistants, email filtering, recommendation systems, healthcare text analysis, and legal document processing.

Common NLP libraries include NLTK, spaCy, scikit-learn, TensorFlow, PyTorch, and Hugging Face Transformers.

The future of NLP includes more human-like conversations, multilingual AI systems, better context understanding, integration with AI agents, and more explainable models.
