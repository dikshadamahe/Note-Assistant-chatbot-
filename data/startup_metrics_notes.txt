Monthly Recurring Revenue, or MRR, is the predictable revenue a startup expects every month from active subscriptions.
Annual Recurring Revenue, or ARR, is calculated as MRR multiplied by 12.
If MRR is 40 lakhs, ARR is approximately 4.8 crores.
Revenue and MRR are not always the same because one-time payments are part of revenue but not part of MRR.
Gross Revenue includes subscription revenue, one-time setup charges, and service fees.
Net Revenue is gross revenue minus refunds, discounts, and cancellations.
Customer Acquisition Cost, or CAC, is the average amount spent to acquire one paying customer.
CAC is calculated by dividing total sales and marketing cost by the number of new customers acquired.
Customer Lifetime Value, or LTV, estimates the total revenue expected from a customer during their relationship with the company.
A healthy SaaS business often targets an LTV to CAC ratio greater than 3.
Churn rate is the percentage of customers who cancel their subscription during a period.
Revenue churn measures the percentage of recurring revenue lost from churned customers.
Net Revenue Retention, or NRR, measures how much recurring revenue is retained including expansions and contractions.
An NRR above 100 percent means expansion revenue is greater than lost revenue.
Gross margin is calculated as revenue minus cost of goods sold, divided by revenue.
Burn rate is the amount of cash a startup spends every month.
Runway is the number of months a startup can survive with current cash balance and burn rate.
If a company has 80 lakhs in cash and a monthly burn of 10 lakhs, runway is 8 months.
EBITDA stands for earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization.
Contribution margin is revenue minus variable costs, and it shows how much each sale contributes to fixed costs.
Average Revenue Per User, or ARPU, is total recurring revenue divided by active paying users.
Conversion rate is the percentage of leads that become paying customers.
A low conversion rate may indicate weak product messaging or poor onboarding.
Activation rate measures how many new users reach a key value milestone in the product.
Retention rate is the percentage of users who remain active after a specific period.
Daily Active Users and Monthly Active Users are engagement metrics often shown as DAU and MAU.
The DAU to MAU ratio is used to estimate product stickiness.
A contribution margin below zero means each additional customer increases losses.
Break-even point is when total revenue equals total costs.
Cash flow from operations should be monitored separately from accounting profit.
Operating leverage improves when fixed costs stay stable while revenue grows.
Unit economics describe whether the business model is profitable at the customer level.
Sales efficiency measures how effectively sales spend generates new recurring revenue.
Payback period is the time required to recover CAC from gross profit generated by a customer.
Revenue recognition should follow accounting standards and may differ from cash collection timing.
Forecast accuracy improves when finance teams track pipeline conversion and churn assumptions.
Scenario planning should include best-case, base-case, and worst-case revenue projections.
A startup dashboard should include MRR, ARR, churn, CAC, LTV, runway, and gross margin at minimum.
